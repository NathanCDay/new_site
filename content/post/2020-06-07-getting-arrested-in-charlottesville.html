Intro

It’s been inspiring to see the world unite in protest against systemic police racism. It’s depressing because our country claims to stand for something better. To make this massive issue feel closer, I analyzed the arrest records from my city’s police department here in Charlottesville, Virginia.

The city’s stats

Using population data from the US Census and arrest data reported by the police, I filtered down to only consider individuals identified as Black or White.

The arrest record data includes all of the charges against an individual at their time of arrest. It is common for a single arrest event to have multiple charges associated with it. So for an example home burglery there would be two charges: Breaking & Entering plus Petty Theft. By assessing multiple charges it increases the likelihood that one of them will lead to a conviction at trial. I’m reporting individual charges in this post and I think that is a fair metric to estimate the impact of policing, but I want to be transparent.

The volume of charges is shocking and the disparity is stark. There have been more charges against Black people (8,888) in the last five years, than there are Black citizens (8,437)!!! I am floored by these numbers, maybe I shouldn’t be because the Bureau of Justic Statistics estimates 1 out of 3 Black men born in America in 2001 will be incarcerated at some point in their lives.

Over time

This split in the number of charges between Black and White people is constant since the data begins in 2015. Even as total charges seem to be trending down (the most recent data points as outliers attributed to COVID-19) the disparity remains.

Using the criminal justice to keep Black people subjugated is an American tradition, the 13th Amendment explicitly allows slavery when it’s used as criminal punishments. Profiting from unpaid convict labor is modern day slavery and it is still happening in America.

Types of charges

There are 478 unique statutes (“laws”) represented in these records. So to summarize the general type of charge, I created a new column capturing the first lowercase word of statute’s long form description.

Looking at the top 20 most frequent charges.

Stark differences between races exists and vary dramatically depending on the statute group. The “firearm” (86%), “credit” (76%), and “lic” (75%) groups are the most biased towards Black people. The “generic” (28%), “illegal” (29%) and “profane” (35%) statues have the smallest fractions of Black people, but all are still biased, remember only 22% of Charlottesville population is Black.

I think it’s insane that “monument” makes the list, considering the neo-nazi rally here 3 years on A12. Most of the “historical” statues in the South were erected during the peak of Jim Crow, they were and are 100% racially motivated symbols.

Notable descriptions that were not immediately apparent for me:

  • profane ~ public intoxication
  • fta/capias ~ failure to appear in courts / arrest on site
  • lic - license suspended/revoked

Catching multiple cases

The 16,103 charges in this data set are attributed to 5,782 people, so a lot of individuals have received multiple charges.

We fail all of our citizens when recidivism is high, but this failure is disproportionately felt by Black citizens. Repeat offenders are failures of the community. We should be be empowering people new skills, instead of recycling them through a system we know is dysfunctional.

To make it even worse most of the charges against these people are non-violent. So we are keeping a lot of people cycling through the system for minor issues that don’t directly hurt anyone else.
